📐Framework

MPBP Operational Governance

Map, Prioritize, Build, Pilot. A practitioner's sequence for operationalizing existing AI governance standards.

View details ↓
MapInventory systems, use cases, vendors, decision points, and stakeholders before building governance.
PrioritizeClassify use cases by impact. Align oversight proportionally. Not all AI creates the same risk.
BuildAdapt existing structures (NIST, OECD, EU AI Act) to your legal, operational, and organizational context.
PilotBuild around an actual deployment, then refine. Governance designed in isolation rarely survives implementation.

⚖️Architecture

Risk-Tiered Governance Architecture

Capability boundaries calibrated to deployment risk context, not one-size-fits-all policy documents.

View details ↓
Read-Only TierInformation retrieval, search, analysis. Highest autonomy, lightest constraints.
Reversible TierDraft creation, internal routing, non-binding recommendations.
Irreversible TierFinancial transactions, data modifications, binding commitments. Human-authority gates required.
External-Facing TierCustomer-facing, regulatory, or public-facing actions. Maximum constraint, mandatory approval.

🏗️Methodology

Five Layers of Operational Governance

Minimum viable constraint at maximum operational confidence. Each layer is load-bearing, testable, independently valuable.

View details ↓
01. Risk-Tiered Capability BoundariesDefine what each agent can and cannot do, calibrated to actual risk profile.
02. Constraint EncodingGovernance rules as code. Testable, versioned, and auditable.
03. Human-Authority MechanismsRisk-classified gating. Autonomous where earned.
04. Behavioral MonitoringRuntime observation. Detect drift before incident.
05. Policy Versioning & TestingGovernance that evolves. Find where constraints break first.
